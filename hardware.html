Hardware

What is MIDI?

MIDI stands for 'Musical Instrument Digital Interface'. It is a way for you to interact with your DAW to record in music from the device, or to play and place in notes.


The keyboard featured on the left is the very popular AKAI MPK mini. It is a very simple keyaobrd to use, and convinient to use because of it's size. As visible, there are drum pads at the top, various dials on the left to tweak and adjust settings and then the keyboard keys below.

Other kinds of hardware

Hardware used to make music does not have to connect to the system directly. As visible in the image on the right you can have various acoustic instruments such as guitars, drums and even synthesizers which can pass audio into your computer either via a microphone or audio interface.


Using analog hardware gives an authentic sound that is sometimes impossible to recreate using digital instruments, which is the main advantage of them.
